Career Timeline

My professional path connects molecular biology, infectious disease research, genetic toxicology, long-read sequencing, bioinformatics automation, and regulatory science. The current focus is error-corrected NGS for ultra-rare mutation detection and safety assessment.

Biologist

U.S. FDA National Center for Toxicological Research (NCTR), Division of Genetic and Molecular Toxicology

Jefferson, AR | January 2020 - Present

• Serve as a subject-matter expert in HiFi/PacBio sequencing for genetic toxicology, developing and optimizing error-corrected NGS assays for ultra-rare somatic mutation detection at frequencies as low as ≤1×10-7 mutations per base pair.

• Named Co-Investigator in 2024 on two FDA-approved projects: Evaluation of HiFi Sequencing Against Established Genetic Toxicology Assays and Next Generation Safety Assessment of Advanced Therapies Incorporating Genome Editing.

• Design and maintain automated bioinformatics pipelines using Linux, Bash, Python, R, BWA, Samtools, Bedtools, Bcftools, VarScan, Picard, and pbmm2 for Illumina and PacBio BAM/FASTA/VCF analysis, mutation calling, and mutational spectrum visualization.

• Reduced NGS analysis time by approximately 20% through Bash/Python/R automation and developed a GUI-based, Singularity-containerized PacBio HiFi workflow for reproducible analysis by non-command-line users.

• Integrated LLM and AI tools, including Ollama and Jupyter AI, into genomic research workflows for literature mining, interpretation support, and workflow generation.

• Train and mentor researchers in DNA extraction, PacBio/Illumina library preparation, sequencing operations, and bioinformatics; manage weekly library preparation, instrument calibration, supply procurement, and an annual lab budget of approximately $70,000.

• Maintain L5178Y and TK6 cell models; conduct CRISPR base-editor off-target studies, and in vitro genotoxicity studies involving mutagenic agents.
